The Long Term Consequences of Student Loan Debt in the United States

And Why It Matters

Portfolio

About

Student debt eats at your income, ten to twenty percent of the average person's income is used to repay student loan debt. Current students and recent graduates are having to put their future lives on hold for student debt. These graduates are having to place a hold on having children, owning a home, and their future goals. “Indebted college graduates have lower net worth, less home equity, and a compromised ability to accumulate assets compared to their peers with the same level of education but no student debt” ( Elliot and Lewis). The benefits of attending a postsecondary education seems to be uneven for those who are fighting off student loan debt. Newly graduated students that are paying off student debt will begin to accumulate assets once they obtain a job and will begin to become financially stable, but when student loan debt must be repaid, the assets that have been accumulated will begin to diminish for years following the completion of a degree. Student loan debt brings on a great deal of financial stress and strain to the many students that have borrowed money for their education. There are many negative consequences for borrowing money to pay for postsecondary education, student paying back student debt experiences delays in purchasing a home, or are forced to buy a home with a higher interest rate, these students must put their retirement funds or other investments on hold until all their funds are paid back. It was found that students fighting off student debt will have accumulated $100,000 less in retirement than a student that was not faced with any student loans. Student debt also impacts the children of the graduates that are faced with the student debt. Families paying off student debt have confessed that their student loans have had a negative effect on their ability to provide for their children or pay for child care.

THE NIGHTMARE OF STUDENT LOANS

Young adults and adults are now more than ever faced with a problem that is very hard to escape, student loans. Throughout our lives many are encouraged to continue their academic career by attending college. As the majority do so, a lot of these people are then faced with having major students. debt. There are people who are fortunate enough to be able to come out of college without any debt but that isn't the situation that many people end up being in. Whether it's a couple thousand or tens of thousands of dollars that people end up with, it will affect that person for many years moving forward. Having to continue to pay for your loans after graduating can take many people years and years to do and they continue to have that dollar amount dangling in front of them as they continue to struggle paying it off. Whether it affects you or not, student loans are something that need to be taken care of and controlled better.

The statistical image explains that over half of student taking loans do not know how long their loan payments will continue for, the students are unaware of how much they will be paying back each month for their student loans, the students do not know how much of their income will be put toward paying off student debt, and student don't know if anyone co signed their loan, meaning that they are faced with the financial burden of paying back student debt alone, which will in turn may negatively affect their future home status, their children, and their future income.

The Weight of Student Loan Debts

Students and recent graduates are having to put their financial and life goals on hold as they repay their student loan debts. This can have a negative impact on one’s mental health. As stated in the article I chose to research, "PwC’s 2017 Employee Financial Wellness Survey showed that 45% of millennials and 42% of Gen X respondents felt that their student loan debt was negatively affecting their ability to achieve their financial goals”(Kim and Chatterjee, 2019).

Effects of Student Debt

This image illustrates how student debt affects students, specifically graduates negatively. Student debt weighs graduates down and follows them as they leave their college. Most graduates leave school still with debt which effects their person life outside of school. For example, it effects their ability to buy a house and for jobs, they often have to apply for lower-skilled and lower-paying jobs in order to pay their loan bills right away no matter their qualifications. This can be overwhelming because graduates do not receive the benefits of their college degree.
